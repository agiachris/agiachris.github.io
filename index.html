Christopher George Agia

I am a fourth year Engineering Science student at the University of Toronto, majoring in Robotics Engineering and minoring in Business. I'm in the process of completing my Professional Experience Year (PEY) as an autonomous systems researcher at Huawei Noah's Ark Research Lab, where I work on deep learning modules for self-driving vehicles.

I'm also an active member of aUToronto (UofT Self-driving Car Group), where I am developing a real-time 3D detection and tracking pipeline with the Object Detection Team.

I enjoy working in team environments as I believe that the best ideas are generated through group discussion and collaboration. I value work ethic, creativity, and forward thinking.

Research

My research interests lie in the fields of Computer Vision, Deep Learning, and Robotics. Currently, I am focused on learning-based LiDAR/multi-sensor perception, but I also have strong interests in unsupervised learning methods such as Deep Reinforcement Learning and Generative Models.

I have previous research experience at the Autonomous Systems Lab working under the supervision of Prof. Goldie Nejat. During my stay, I worked with a team of graduate researchers to investigate the application of Deep Reinforcement Learning for autonomous robot navigation in rough terrain.

Sim2Real: Deep Reinforcement Learning for Autonomous Robot Navigation in Rough Terrain
Kaicheng Zhang, Han Hu, Aaron Hao Tan, Michael Ruan, Christopher Agia, Goldie Nejat
ICRA 2020.

We use Deep Reinforcement Learning with Transfer Learning techniques to approach the Sim2Real challenge, enabling a robot to learn suitable navigation behaviour for rough terrain.
